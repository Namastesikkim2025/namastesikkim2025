Arts & Crafts of Sikkim

A journey into the cultural heritage of the Himalayas

Thangka Paintings

Thangkas are intricate Buddhist paintings on cotton or silk, representing deities and spiritual stories, often used for meditation.

Wooden Carvings

Skilled artisans of Sikkim create beautiful wooden carvings seen in monasteries, furniture, and decorative items.

Handicrafts

From bamboo works to woolen carpets, Sikkim’s handicrafts reflect deep cultural significance and eco-friendly artistry.

Paper Mâché Art

Colorful masks, toys, and ritual objects made using traditional paper mâché techniques.

Lepcha Weaving

Traditional handwoven clothes of the Lepcha tribe, using natural dyes and geometric patterns.

Stone Carvings

Stone-carved statues and inscriptions, often seen in monasteries and sacred sites.

Traditional Jewellery

Beautiful gold, silver, and turquoise ornaments crafted by local artisans, worn during festivals.

Basketry

Handwoven baskets and storage containers made from bamboo, cane, and reeds.

Applique Work

Colorful fabric applique used for monastery decorations and ceremonial textiles.

Silk Weaving

Fine silk textiles woven with Buddhist motifs and traditional patterns.

Traditional Dolls

Handcrafted dolls in traditional attire, often made from cloth, wood, and clay.
