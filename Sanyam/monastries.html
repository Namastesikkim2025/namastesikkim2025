Sacred Monasteries

Discover the spiritual heart of Sikkim through its ancient monasteries.
Experience centuries of Buddhist wisdom, stunning architecture, and peaceful serenity.

Rumtek Monastery

History: Rumtek Monastery, also known as the Dharma Chakra Centre, is a prominent Tibetan Buddhist monastery near Gangtok, Sikkim. Built originally in the mid-1700s, rebuilt in the 1960s by the 16th Karmapa.

Cultural Significance: Main seat of the Karma Kagyu lineage, holds relics, and is a crucial pilgrimage site.

Gangtok, Sikkim

Pemayangtse Monastery

History: One of the oldest and most important monasteries in Sikkim, founded in 1705 by Lhatsun Chempo, belonging to the Nyingma order of Tibetan Buddhism.

Cultural Significance: Known for its seven-tiered wooden sculpture, tradition, and annual festivals.

Pelling, West Sikkim

Tashiding Monastery

History: Founded in 1717 by Ngadak Sempa Chempo, a disciple of Guru Padmasambhava.

Cultural Significance: Famous for the annual Bhumchu Festival and sacred holy water.

West Sikkim

Enchey Monastery

History: Established in 1909 by Lama Drupthob Karpo, known for his tantric powers.

Cultural Significance: Famous for Cham dances during religious festivals.

Gangtok, East Sikkim

Phodong Monastery

History: Built in the early 18th century by Chogyal Gyurmed Namgyal.

Cultural Significance: A major Kagyu monastery, hosts Cham dances and holds ancient murals.

North Sikkim

Ralang Monastery

History: Established in 1768 by the 4th Chogyal after his pilgrimage to Tibet.

Cultural Significance: Hosts the grand Pang Lhabsol and Mahakala dance festivals.

South Sikkim

Lingdum (Ranka) Monastery

History: A relatively new monastery, built in the late 20th century, designed in typical Tibetan style.

Cultural Significance: Known for its architecture and being a major training centre for young monks.

Near Gangtok, East Sikkim

Dubdi Monastery

History: Built in 1701, it is considered the oldest monastery in Sikkim, founded by Lhatsun Namkha Jigme.

Cultural Significance: Known as the "Hermit’s Cell," it holds historic scriptures and relics.

Yuksom, West Sikkim

Phensang Monastery

History: Established in 1721 by Lama Jigme Pawo, belonging to the Nyingma order.

Cultural Significance: Hosts the annual Cham dance festival before Losoong (Sikkimese New Year).

North Sikkim
