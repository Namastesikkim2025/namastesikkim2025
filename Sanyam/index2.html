About Rumtek Monastery

Rumtek Monastery, also known as the Dharma Chakra Centre, stands as one of the most important monasteries in Sikkim. It serves as the seat of the Karmapa Lama, the head of the Kagyu sect of Tibetan Buddhism.

Built in the 16th century and rebuilt later, Rumtek is famous for its grand prayer hall, golden stupa, and rare Buddhist art objects. It continues to be a center for spiritual learning and meditation.

The monastery is surrounded by scenic hills, offering a peaceful environment that attracts both devotees and travelers seeking tranquility.
